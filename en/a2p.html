A2P Message Delivery Platform

A high-throughput delivery management platform was developed to support authentication and transactional message delivery across multiple communication channels. Supported delivery methods include push notifications, SMS, Flash Calls, voice calls (with TTS), Telegram, and WhatsApp. Most integrations are implemented directly with telecom and messaging providers.

Key requirements included: on-premises deployment within customer infrastructure; support for cascading message delivery; high-throughput push notification pipeline with minimal latency.

Implementation Details

The services are implemented in Golang, with Kafka and gRPC as transport layers. Each delivery channel has configurable worker pools, concurrency limits, and retry policies. Cascading delivery logic is implemented to automatically re-route messages through fallback channels.

The platform is integrated into an internal CI/CD and IaC. It exposes detailed metrics covering service health, device-level delivery stats, latency per provider, and failure diagnostics.

The push notification service integrates with APNS, FCM, and Huawei Push Kit. It manages message encoding, dispatching, and response handling. The service is optimized for high throughput and low latency, suitable for real-time messaging systems.

SMS and voice delivery services support integration with internal VoIP infrastructure, communication PaaS providers, and mobile network operators. Voice calls are generated using an in-house text-to-speech engine. Delivery failures are handled by fallback logic and integrated with external services to maintain phone number validity and update records.
